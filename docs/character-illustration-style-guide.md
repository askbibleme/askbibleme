# Character Illustration Style Guide

Approved master style: `Style 1`

This project's biblical character illustrations and chapter scene images should follow one consistent visual language:

- Classical biblical oil painting
- Dramatic candlelit chiaroscuro
- Warm amber-brown earth palette
- Emotional realism with restrained expression
- Soft aged canvas texture
- Painterly rendering, but not loose or abstract
- Asymmetrical narrative composition with clear foreground/background separation
- Modest historical interiors or landscapes with simple biblical-era props

Required consistency rules:

- Every new character image must feel like it belongs to the same painted series
- Keep lighting low-key and warm across the whole collection
- Keep skin rendering, facial realism, drapery weight, and brush texture consistent
- Preserve biblical-era materials: wool, linen, leather, simple wood, stone, clay
- Avoid drifting into flatter, cleaner, brighter, or more neutral illustration styles

Do:

- Use deep shadows and focused warm light
- Favor intimate narrative moments over generic posed portraits
- Maintain costume variety without breaking historical plausibility
- Keep expressions subtle and solemn

Do not:

- Use anime, cartoon, comic, or stylized fantasy looks
- Use glossy modern cinematic color grading
- Use bright fantasy colors or clean digital concept-art rendering
- Use flat white studio backgrounds, decorative frames, or sticker-like cutouts
- Let later generations drift toward cleaner, lighter, or more balanced editorial illustration

Reusable style direction:

```text
Use Style 1 as the master art direction for all generated character illustrations. Maintain a consistent classical biblical oil-painting look across the full series: dramatic candlelit chiaroscuro, warm earth-tone palette, deep shadows, aged canvas texture, emotional realism, asymmetrical narrative composition, soft painterly rendering, restrained expressions, simple historical interiors, and no modern glossy digital-art aesthetics. Every new character image must feel like it belongs to the same painted collection.
```

Hard anti-drift rule:

```text
All future character illustrations must match this approved reference style and should not drift toward flatter, cleaner, brighter, or more neutral illustration styles.
```
